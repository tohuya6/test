Key Concepts and Equations

Charging Methods

Objects gain or lose electric charge through specific processes, each with distinct mechanisms and outcomes:

  • Friction: Electrons transfer between materials due to contact and separation, leaving one object positively charged and the other negatively charged. For example, rubbing a glass rod with silk transfers electrons to the silk, leaving the glass rod positively charged and the silk negatively charged.
  • Conduction: Direct physical contact transfers electrons between objects, typically from a charged object to a conductor or vice versa.
  • Induction: A charged object induces charge separation in a nearby conductor without direct contact.

Coulomb's Law

Fₑ = k (|q₁q₂| / r²)

Conditions of Use: Applies to point charges or spherical charge distributions treated as point charges, in a vacuum or air (where k = 8.99 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²), assuming charges are stationary.

Applications: Calculate the electrostatic force between two point charges, analyze attraction or repulsion in atomic or molecular systems.

Electric Fields

Point Charge:

E = k (|q| / r²)

Continuous Distributions:

E = (1 / 4πε₀) ∫ (dq / r²)
